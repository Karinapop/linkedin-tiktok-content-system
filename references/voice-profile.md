### Structure and storytelling (what actually makes a post land)

Capture the shape of your best-performing or favorite post here. Generic patterns that work for most people, keep the ones that are true for you:

- **Quality and storytelling beat brevity. Length is not a constraint.** A longer post is fine, even preferred, when the story is stronger for it. Never trim a good story to hit a length. Never pad a thin one.
- **Lead with one story told all the way through.** Pick the single best concrete example and tell it with a small arc (setup, what you did, the specific result, the turn). Then layer other examples more briefly. Don't open with a flat equal-weight bullet list.
- **Use a staccato fragment list for rhythm** where it fits. Short noun-phrase fragments, each on its own line, to build feeling.
- **The humility reframe.** When you accomplish something impressive, credit the friction removal or the tool, not just your own talent. Keeps you relatable.
- **Ground every flex, no embellishment.** Real and specific beats impressive and inflated, always. Concrete numbers over vague superlatives.
- **State the insight plainly in the middle, then let it breathe.**

## Hard rules (these override voice mimicry)

These are the defaults this skill ships with. Keep them unless you have a strong reason to change one for your own brand.

- **No em dashes or en dashes, ever.** Replace with a period, colon, comma, or parentheses. This is non-negotiable and the easiest rule to leak on a long generation. Sweep for `—` and `–` before saving.
- **Sentence case.** Capitalize sentence starts and proper nouns. Never all-lowercase, never Title Case sentences.
- **No hashtags on LinkedIn** by default. (Hashtags are fine on TikTok captions, where they're platform-native.)
- **First line must earn the scroll-stop.** Lead with a revelation, a sharp opinion, or a recognizable name/number. Generic category openers ("Most marketers...") don't count.
- **Never exaggerate, never borrow examples that aren't yours.** Do not inflate claims, do not invent or import examples, do not stretch the truth for a punchier line. Use your real, specific details exactly as given. If the true version is smaller, write the smaller true version. Relatable and honest beats impressive and fake, every time.
- **Casual and raw over polished, on both platforms.** Even LinkedIn posts should read closer to how you text and talk than to a "written" thought-leadership post. Shorter sentences, plain words, a little unpolished. TikTok is even more casual, but don't overcorrect into forced slang.
