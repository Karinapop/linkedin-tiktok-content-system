# TikTok Script Format

The TikTok deliverable is a short, punchy, shootable script. The bar is a script a real creator would say out loud to a friend. Here is the energy to match (a worked example):

> "AI productivity this. AI productivity that. Nobody is talking about using AI to live more intentionally. This past week I designed a couple's gag hoodie, made a slideshow game for a trip, and designed custom coffee cups. Life has never been more personalizable than with AI."
>
> Caption: Thank you AI tools 😘

Study what that is and isn't. It's about 45 words, maybe 18 seconds. A snappy hook, a rapid real-example list, one fun landing line, a playful caption that tags the tools. It is NOT a 45-second, five-beat shot list with b-roll columns. Match this energy.

## Length is the #1 rule: keep it short

- Target **15 to 30 seconds** of talking. Roughly **40 to 70 words**. When in doubt, cut.
- 15-30s clips have the highest completion rates and get shared and rewatched most. 21-34s drives the most likes and shares. The algorithm rewards videos watched to the end, so short wins.
- If a draft runs past ~70 words, it's too long. Brevity IS the format here, the opposite of the LinkedIn posts where length is free.

## Voice: talk like a friend

This still matters most. The spoken lines should sound like the user talking to a friend, not announcing to an audience.
- Open mid-conversation, use real reactions, talk to one person ("you"), contractions everywhere.
- **The user's own playful slang is great.** Avoid generic creator clichés ("main character," "superior," "it's giving") unless the user actually talks that way.
- Same hard rules as LinkedIn carry over: no em dashes, sentence case, translate jargon to everyday value, never exaggerate or borrow examples that aren't theirs.

## Structure (lightweight, not a shot list)

Deliver these, in order:

1. **Hook (first ~3 seconds).** One punchy line. A strong formula is **mock the cliché with repetition, then pivot**: "AI productivity this. AI productivity that. Nobody's talking about [the real angle]." Other good hook angles: a plain revelation, a surprising number, a "nobody's talking about X," a **PSA** ("PSA: that project you keep pushing off, AI can probably just do it now"), a **POV**, and a **list-tease** ("things I made with AI this week that have nothing to do with my job"). By default offer **3 to 4 alternative hooks per idea** in different angles, not just one. Keep one as the recommended lead and list the rest as alternates.
2. **The script.** The actual words to say, tight. A rapid list of REAL examples is a great body. No per-second timestamps, no b-roll columns needed.
3. **Light visual note (optional).** One or two quick cues at most (e.g., "flash the product as you say it"). Keep it minimal. Don't choreograph every second.
4. **Caption.** Short and playful, one line. Can thank or tag the tools used.
5. **Hashtags.** 3 to 5, a mix of broad and niche.
6. **Sound.** See below.

Only break out a longer beat-by-beat table if the idea genuinely needs choreography (a reveal, a multi-scene bit). Default to the tight script.

## Sound: match the format honestly, don't over-recommend layering

Trending audio drives real reach (posts with it get meaningfully more views and reach), but HOW you use it depends on the video type. Don't reflexively recommend a trending song under every talking-head clip. That advice is only right in specific cases.

- **Lifestyle / b-roll clips with little talking** (showing a product, a get-ready montage): a trending sound can carry the whole video. This is where trending audio pays off most. Recommend one.
- **Talking-head clips** (most of these scripts): the voice IS the content, and platforms boost original audio on its own. Layering a song is optional and only helps if it's INSTRUMENTAL or low-energy and sits quiet (roughly 5-15% volume) under the voice. Never recommend a lyric-heavy song under talking, the lyrics fight the words and tank comprehension and watch-time.
- **Serious or emotional talking-heads**: recommend original audio, or at most a soft ambient bed very low. A clean voice lands harder than a pop song under it.
- **Timing if you do ride a trend:** a sound used within ~48 hours of taking off gets 3 to 5x more push, and trends peak in 5 to 14 days. So never name a specific track (it'll be stale by shoot time). Say "grab whatever [vibe] sound is trending the day you post."
- **Priority reminder for talking-head reach:** hook (first 2 seconds) > retention and pacing > captions and on-screen text > the sound. The song is a nice-to-have, not the lever. Say so rather than overselling audio.

## Posting time (guidance, the user posts these themselves)

TikTok's best windows differ from LinkedIn's morning slot. Aim **Tuesday to Thursday, late morning to early afternoon ET (roughly 11am to 1pm)**, or **Sunday morning**. Posting a bit before the audience's peak lets the video bank early engagement before primetime. These are scripts the user shoots and posts manually, so this is guidance, not an auto-schedule.
